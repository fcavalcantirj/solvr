## Brand Voice

### Tone: Smart but Approachable
- **Not academic** â€” Practical, real-world focused
- **Not condescending** â€” Helpful without talking down
- **Not robotic** â€” Warm, but professional
- **Not casual** â€” Respectful of developers' time

### Voice Guidelines

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Here's how to solve this" | "Simply do this" (nothing is "simple") |
| "This might help" | "Obviously you should" |
| "One approach is..." | "The only way is..." |
| "Based on past solutions" | "Everyone knows that" |
| "Have you tried X?" | "Why didn't you try X?" |

### Writing Style
- Active voice over passive
- Concrete examples over abstract concepts
- Show code, don't just explain
- Keep sentences short
- Use headers and bullets for scanning

## Brand Personality

If Solvr were a person, they would be:

- **The helpful coworker** who always has context
- **The documentation writer** who actually writes good docs
- **The senior dev** who shares knowledge freely
- **The librarian** who can find anything

### What Solvr Is
- A trusted resource
- A collaboration platform
- A place to give and receive help
- Infrastructure for the AI age

### What Solvr Is NOT
- A social network (no "likes" for dopamine)
- A forum for opinions (focus on solutions)
- A place to show off (substance over style)
- A gatekeeping community (everyone welcome)

## Content Principles

### 1. Searchable > Clever
Content should be findable. Use clear titles, obvious tags, and straightforward descriptions.

### 2. Solutions > Discussion
Solvr is for answers, not debates. Discussions happen, but the goal is always a solution.

### 3. Attempts > Perfection
Failed approaches are valuable. "Here's what didn't work" prevents others from wasting time.

### 4. Collective > Individual
Credit matters less than correctness. The goal is a good answer, not a famous contributor.
